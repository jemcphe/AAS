Social Behaviors

Every Culture has a diverse group of individuals. Through the many different individuals, however, there are many similar actions and cultural norms. Below are cultural norms that you will notice as you travel through Scotland.

Greetings

Universal Greeting - When greeting each other, Men shake hands with a “How do you do?”. Meetings between men and women are the same, however, the woman generally extend their hand first.

Communication Style

  • The people of Scotland are very polite. Politeness is highly valued in Scotland. You can expect a “please”, thank you”, and a “sorry”, if need-be, while in Scotland.
  • Scots are not very direct, especially in moments where most might have a complaint. Most Scottish people would “brush” their shoulders off, and move on.
  • Scots are very spirited and enthusiastic when talking. The tone of voice and facial expressions are an important part of Scottish communication.

Personal Space

  • The people of Scotland generally prefer 2 – 3 feet between them while speaking.
  • Touching another individual is kept to a minimum unless you know them very well.

Eye Contact

Eye contact is important in Scotland. It shows that you are attentive and engaging in a meaningful conversation. Eye contact is a sign of respect and being polite.

Views of Time

As in American Culture, punctuality is very important. When meeting for business, especially. It is disrespectful in any culture to keep someone waiting for you to show up.

Gestures

  • In Scotland, giving the “peace sign” is seen as an obscenity.
  • In America, we generally walk on the right and pass on the left. This is inverted in Scotland. People walk on the left and pass on the right.
